Why a routine, not a course

You don't learn your way out of a paralyzed gut. You practice your way out.

A sick, methane-slowed gut is a muscle that has forgotten how to sweep itself clean between meals. You can't fix that with information. You fix it the way you'd rehabilitate any muscle — with the same deliberate movements, repeated, day after day, until the body takes over on its own.

So INTO THE ROUTINE is not a wellness seminar. There are no long lectures to sit through. There is a rhythm — a single, proven healing day built from one man's eight-year recovery from a once-life-threatening case — and you simply live it. Wake, walk, breathe, plunge, eat, move, rest. Then do it again tomorrow. And the morning after that. Seven times, in a place quiet enough for your nervous system to finally let go.

This is a motility disease wearing a digestion costume. Kill all you want — if the gut doesn't sweep itself clean between meals, it grows back. The routine is what makes the change stick.
The heart of it

The daily rhythm — the same, every day

This is the whole product: one carefully sequenced day, repeated for seven mornings. The logic is "the motility cascade" — disrupt at dawn, eat in a tight window, fast long, let the night run clean. Cold lands while you're warm-bodied and fed; the deep motility work lands in the late-afternoon fast.

What you'll actually do

The practices

Six disciplines, woven into every day. Each is gentle, scalable, supervised, and screened for who should sit it out. Nothing here is high-intensity — a reset is built on consistency, not punishment.

🧊

Cold immersion

Brief, dosed cold — built from the founder's own discovery that cold water calmed the burning and pressure when nothing else would. Vagal reset and relief, not endurance. Buddy system, strict time limits, long exhales, fully optional. Beginners welcome; nobody is pushed.

🌬️

Breathwork

The 4-in/4-out lower-belly breath that releases the rectosigmoid angle and tones the vagus nerve — plus a "rest-and-digest" drill before every meal and a wind-down practice at night. Slow and even-paced; no forceful holds.

🧘

Gut-directed movement & yoga

Knees-to-chest, happy-baby, supported squat, legs-up-the-wall, gentle twists — sequences chosen to move trapped gas and coax the colon. Plus a supervised "inversion" to release the angle. Restorative, not a workout.

♻️

Motility & clearance

The morning ginger ritual, post-meal walks, abdominal self-massage, the no-strain bathroom drill, and the late-afternoon fasted clearance work — all aimed at restarting the gut's own "sweeper" so it carries the load instead of you forcing it.

🍖

The carnivore table

Every meal from our private kitchen: pressure-cooked bone broth, fatty cooked meat and fish, salted and timed — the food that starves the overgrowth and rebuilds the lining. Sit, eat, breathe, walk. (More below.)

🌙

Stillness & rest

Deep sleep treated as infrastructure, evening circles, Epsom-salt baths, a digital sundown, and long unstructured quiet. The nervous system does the repair — so we build the days to let it.

Fed by our private kitchen

The carnivore table

Diet is roughly 90% of the work — so the kitchen is the centre of the house, not an afterthought. Every plate is prepared in-house and built to do one job: starve the methane overgrowth of the fermentable fuel it lives on, while rebuilding the gut lining.

  • Bone broth, daily, as medicine. Pressure-cooked for hours into a rich extract of collagen, glycine and minerals — sipped warm at every turn.
  • Fatty, cooked meat & fish. Ribeye, lamb, beef, salmon — salted heavily, always cooked through. Fat is the fuel while you fat-adapt.
  • The real electrolytes. Not just salt — sodium plus potassium and magnesium, the trio that prevents the "keto-flu" crash. A daily electrolyte bench, no gum-filled powders.
  • A tight eating window. Two main meals and broth, last bite by 16:00 — so the long fast can do its work overnight.
  • Nothing that feeds the overgrowth. No sugar, grains, fermentable fibre, coffee or alcohol for the week. A clean, zero-residue plate that lets the gut rest.
  • Careful reintroductions. Toward the end of the week, cleared guests test one food at a time — the method you take home, so you can keep widening your plate safely.
A note on the food

This is a therapeutic, time-boxed way of eating for the week — a demonstration and a kickstart, not a verdict that you'll eat this way forever. You leave knowing exactly how to run this kitchen, and how to reintroduce foods, at home.

Straight answers

FAQ

Is this medical treatment?

No. INTO THE ROUTINE is a lifestyle and practice retreat, not a clinic. We don't diagnose, prescribe, or administer anything. You arrive with written medical clearance from your own doctor, bring and self-manage your own prescribed medicines, and we guide the daily practices — diet, cold, breath, movement and motility work — around them.

Do I stop my medications?

Never on your own. You continue exactly the plan your own doctor gave you, bring your medicines in their original packaging, and take them yourself. Any change is strictly between you and your prescriber.

Why is every day the same?

Because the gut heals through repetition, not information. The migrating motor complex — the wave that sweeps the small intestine clean — is retrained by a consistent daily rhythm of fasting, movement and rest. So we run the same proven sequence every day until your body starts doing it on its own. You move, breathe, plunge, eat and rest — daily.

How fit or experienced do I need to be?

Not very. The movement is gentle and restorative — walking, gut-directed yoga, breathwork — not high-intensity training, which we deliberately avoid during a reset. Cold, fasting and inversions are dosed to each person, screened, supervised, and always optional. You can scale any day down.

Will I be cured in a week?

No — and anyone promising that is lying. Full recovery from severe methane SIBO/IMO takes months. A week is a launchpad: it gets you fat-adapted, teaches your body the rhythm, and sends you home with a plan to continue with your own doctor. The honest timeline is 90 days minimum, often 180 days to a year, with maintenance after.

What exactly is the food?

A carnivore table from our private kitchen: pressure-cooked bone broth daily, fatty cooked meat and fish, heavy salt with potassium and magnesium, in a window that ends by 16:00. No sugar, grains, fermentable fibre, coffee or alcohol. Reintroductions are tested carefully toward the end of the week — and you take the method home.

What if I can't do cold, or can't fast?

Then you don't. Every discipline is optional and individually screened. Cold can be shortened to a foot-soak or skipped; fasting windows are softened for anyone who shouldn't fast hard. The retreat adapts to you, not the other way around.

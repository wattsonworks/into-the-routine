Guest Workbook

The one thing to understand before you come

There is no medicine that cures SIBO/IMO. There is only a complete change of habits. No pill, no protocol, no week away does it for you. This week installs the rhythm in your body by repetition; whether it holds depends on what you carry home. Come ready to practice, not to be fixed.

This is not medical treatment

INTO THE ROUTINE is a lifestyle and practice retreat with on-site medical supervision for safety. We don't diagnose, prescribe, or administer anything. You keep your own doctor and your own medicines, and run the practices around them.

Pre-arrival checklist

All of these must be done before your place is confirmed:

  • Medical assessment by your own physician, including a spine-integrity X-ray (the week includes impact, terrain and inversions — we confirm your frame can take it).
  • Written medical clearance letter from your doctor (template provided) — including your diagnosis, breath-test values if you have them, and full medication/supplement list.
  • Our health intake questionnaire completed and returned.
  • Signed waivers — informed consent, assumption of risk, scope-of-practice acknowledgement.
  • Travel + medical insurance valid in Israel.
  • All medications & supplements packed in original, labelled packaging, with a written dose schedule.
  • Two emergency contacts given, one reachable during the week.
In the 1–2 weeks before you arrive (with your doctor's OK)

Begin easing off coffee and sugar so the first days hurt less; start hydrating with real electrolytes (sodium + potassium + magnesium glycinate); keep taking exactly what your doctor prescribed. Do not start a hard fast or carnivore at home unsupervised before you come.

Packing list

  • Documents/health: passport, insurance card, signed physician clearance, meds & supplements (labelled) + dose schedule, copy of any breath-test/labs, emergency-contact card; glucometer if diabetic; personal inhaler/EpiPen if needed.
  • Cold immersion: swimwear ×2, quick-dry towel ×2, warm robe/poncho, wool hat + gloves + warm socks for re-warming, water shoes (rocky footing), flip-flops.
  • Movement & terrain: trail/grippy shoes (the Old City is steep and uneven), layers (Safed is cool at ~900 m), sun hat, sunglasses, high-SPF sunscreen, refillable bottle, light daypack.
  • Comfort: loose clothing, journal + pen, donut/coccyx cushion, earplugs/eye mask, yoga mat [provided — host to confirm].
  • Leave at home: alcohol, coffee, sugar/snacks, gum-filled electrolyte powders, "greens"/prebiotic/Triphala/probiotic blends, sea moss, aloe — all counterproductive here.

The disciplines — how to do them

4-in / 4-out belly breath (the angle release). Lie on your side. Breathe only into the lower belly, pushing the area under the navel out. 4 seconds in, 4 seconds out; on each out-breath let the muscles around the lower gut go completely slack for those 4 seconds. Slow, even, repeated. No forceful holds, never near water.

Cold immersion. Ice immersion at ~14°C. Control the first gasp with long exhales; never hold your breath in cold. Short and dosed (built up across the week), always with a buddy and a supervisor, always optional. Re-warm actively afterward (robe, hat, movement) — no hot shower straight after.

Impact & terrain. Rhythmic footfall and the steep, uneven lanes mechanically shake a stalled gut and stimulate peristalsis. Dosed and built up; scaled down or skipped on tired/die-off days. Land softly, breathe, hydrate.

Gut-directed movement. Knees-to-chest + side-to-side rock, happy-baby, supported squat, legs-up-the-wall, gentle twists, cat-cow. Move gas downstream without strain. Inversions are gentle, brief, supervised — never forced, never on a full stomach.

Motility & clearance. Morning ginger (chew, swallow the soft pulp, spit hard fibres, chase with warm water), walk after every meal, gentle clockwise abdominal self-massage, no-strain toilet (feet up, breathe, ≤5–10 min, never push).

The table. Three carnivore meals — ~100g, ≥150g, 150g — with bone broth, salted, plus potassium and magnesium. Last meal 16:00, nothing after 17:00. Eat slowly; six long exhales before each meal; walk after.

House rules & FYIs

  • Never hot water on the belly — heat there relaxes and bloats a gut you're trying to move.
  • Hot showers ≤ 4–5 minutes total. Long hot showers are sedating and counterproductive.
  • End every hot shower with ≥ 1 minute of cold water.
  • Ice plunge ~14°C — cold enough; colder isn't better. Buddy + supervisor; never a breath-hold in water.
  • Last meal 16:00; no food after 17:00. The long fast is the point.
  • No coffee, sugar, grains, fermentable fibre or alcohol for the week.
  • Breathe & position first — never push against a paralyzed gut.
  • Your meds stay yours — your doctor's plan, your packaging, you take them.

Read your body (working model — reassurance, not diagnosis)

  • Smell map: sulfur/rotten-egg = hydrogen-sulfide producers dying; "rotten beef"/putrid = old trapped debris clearing; fading to nothing = healing.
  • Gas types feel different: easy gas passes; methane/H₂S gas feels stuck — reach for breath, position and movement, not force.
  • Trapped gas can cause back pain/sciatica — the distended gut presses the spine; it tracks your bloating.
  • New night sweats = your autonomic system and metabolism waking up (watch for true fever >38°C, which is medical).
  • A burp-storm that then stops = the upper gut clearing.
Tell the on-site medical lead / call 101 (Israel) immediately if

Chest pain, fainting, palpitations, confusion · numbness/tingling or seizure · uncontrollable vomiting or inability to keep fluids down · severe or new abdominal pain · watery diarrhoea >3×/day with fever · true fever >38°C. None of these are "push through it."

Mindset that is medicine

  • "Not yet beats too soon" — don't reintroduce a food or rush a stage until you're truly ready.
  • Strength masks severity — feeling strong doesn't mean cleared; respect the caps.
  • Finite backlog — the rot is limited, not infinite; every cleared pocket is one less.
  • Adjust, don't abandon — a hard day or a slip is data, not failure.
  • Quiet gut is the goal — protect the calm after movement; that's where healing happens.

After the retreat — the honest timeline

Keto/adaptation flu in weeks 1–2 → energy returns weeks 3–4 → section-by-section clearance → 90 days minimum, often 180 days to a year for full resolution → 3–6 months of maintenance. Recovery is non-linear; bad days return and pass.

  • Keep the daily rhythm: morning breath, pre-meal exhales, wind-down breath, post-meal walks, the eating window.
  • Continue your doctor's plan (prokinetic/antimicrobials as prescribed); keep cold and impact as your doctor allows.
  • Reintroduce foods one variable at a time, 48-hour observation each.
  • Re-test with a triple-gas breath test (H₂, CH₄, H₂S) — don't declare victory on feeling alone.
  • Take your one-page 90-day plan to your own doctor.
🆘 If the bigger fight gets hard

This condition is isolating. If you're struggling emotionally or having thoughts of self-harm: in Israel call ERAN 1201 (24/7, free, confidential); emergency 101. You're not alone, and it's not weakness.

Part 2 · internal

Host Operations Manual

Scope of practice (read aloud Day 1, signed at intake)

  • Peer-led educational/lifestyle experience + on-site medical supervision for safety only. The host shares what he did; he does not prescribe.
  • No medical/nursing/dietetic/psychological/chiropractic care is implied by the host. Any licensed practitioner works under their own license, scope and insurance, treating the guest as their client.
  • No prescription drug or controlled substance is provided, stored, dosed or "topped up" by the retreat. Guests self-manage meds in a room lock-box.
  • No invasive acts (suppositories, enemas, injections incl. peptides) are performed, demonstrated on a person, or assisted.
  • Dangerous extremes from the source (8-cap allicin, 20 suppositories/day, late-night self-experiments) are taught only as cautionary stories, never instructions.

Intake & medical clearance (two gates before a place is confirmed)

Gate A — physician clearance letter confirms: doctor aware of the retreat's activities (low-fermentation/carnivore menu, fasting windows, ~14°C cold immersion, gentle inversions, breathwork, impact/terrain movement on uneven ground at ~900 m); patient medically stable or activities to exclude; diagnosis + breath-test values; full medication/supplement list with "continue your plan"; red-flag conditions; doctor's emergency contact; spine-integrity X-ray reviewed; signature, license, stamp, date.

Gate B — guest intake questionnaire covering: identity + 2 emergency contacts + insurance; the condition (diagnosis, gas type, severity, suppository/laxative use, crisis history); full meds/supplements (flag benzodiazepines, statins/monacolin-K, prucalopride, rifaximin/metronidazole, insulin/GLP-1, anticoagulants, antihypertensives, etc.); cardiometabolic & general medical; cold-specific, inversion/breath/sauna-specific screens; pregnancy + mental-health + suicidality screen; logistics/diet/kosher/mobility; signed scope + waivers.

Hard exclusions (decline, or require specialist activity-specific clearance)

Active suicidal ideation / unstable serious mental illness · pregnancy (active disciplines) · type-1/insulin-dependent or hypo-prone diabetes without endocrinologist clearance · significant cardiac disease, recent event, serious arrhythmia, pacemaker/ICD · uncontrolled hypertension · seizure disorder · current/recent eating disorder · significant kidney/liver disease or acutely unwell · severe unmanaged IMO with crisis-level symptoms (needs a doctor, not a retreat) · spine instability/red flags on X-ray [reviewing clinician's call] · anyone unwilling to clear, sign, or self-manage meds.

On-site emergency plan

  • Numbers posted everywhere (rooms, kitchen, cold area, rooftop, lanyards): Ambulance/medical 101 · Police 100 · Fire 102 · Emotional-crisis ERAN 1201 · nearest ER Ziv Medical Center, Safed [confirm address + drive time] · host mobile · safety-lead mobile · each guest's own-doctor contact (with consent).
  • Daily Safety Lead (sober, phone charged); first-aid/CPR-certified staff on site at all times [hire/confirm]; first-aid kit + AED on site [confirm — strongly recommended with cold].
  • Cold: never alone/unsupervised, never after sedatives/alcohol, trained supervisor present, thermometer + timer + non-slip entry + warm-up station.
  • Meds: each guest's own locked box; nothing shared or dispensed by staff.
  • Vehicle on standby, sober driver, route to Ziv known; villa coordinates pinned for MDA.
  • Incident protocol: Stop → Assess → Call 101 if any doubt → first aid in scope → notify Safety Lead/host/emergency contact → document → review before resuming.

Daily run-of-show checklist (staff)

  • Morning medical check-in logged (HR/BP/weight baseline days 1–2; red-flag screen daily; extra attention on the low-energy/die-off days).
  • Buddy pairs confirmed; per-discipline contraindication re-check called before each session ("anyone with X, sit this one out").
  • Kitchen: 3 meals prepped to portion (100/150/150g) + broth; allergy/kosher sheet on the wall; electrolytes (Na/K/Mg-glycinate, no gums) ready.
  • Cold site: water ~14°C verified, timer/thermometer, non-slip, warm-up station, AED + first-aid kit, supervisor present.
  • Impact/terrain route pre-walked; shade/water/turnaround points known; pace for the slowest.
  • ≤90 min structured movement total for the day (don't let facilitators stack); cold short/optional on die-off days.
  • Evening: journaling + symptom log; digital sundown; guests' own meds + glycine; quiet protected for sleep.
  • Incident log reviewed; tomorrow's adaptations noted (who needs a lighter day).
